01 · Problem

Seoul sets the trend; the world finds out late.

The demand is here — the curated, tasteful guide is not.

  • K-beauty is in a global super-cycle. Korea’s cosmetics exports hit a record $10.2B in 2024 (+20.3%); it overtook France as the #1 US beauty import; it’s been #1 in Japan for three years.
  • The derm angle is a boom. Korea drew 1.17M foreign patients in 2024 — dermatology the #1 specialty at 56.6% — with Japan + China 60% of them. Medical tourism is a decision most people make blind.
  • Discovery is late, fragmented, untranslated. What trends in Seoul (Olive Young, clinic menus, Xiaohongshu — 300M MAU, 87% beauty discovery) reaches the West slowly and in pieces.
  • No premium, multi-market trend guide exists. The audience gets crowd anecdote, mass SEO, or influencers — not a Seoul-native magazine with taste and an MD trust layer.
02 · Solution

A premium magazine for what Seoul is doing now.

Six desks turn fast, tasteful trend curation into a repeatable product — with a light MD ‘Verdict’ and a medical-tourism decision layer.

Trend Radar

The ‘렉카’ core: what’s blowing up in Seoul this week — products, ingredients, looks, clinic menus — with a taste-filtered should-you-care rating.

The Treatment

Derm & procedures explained — lasers, injectables, exosomes, boosters — with the light MD ‘Verdict’ seal.

The Shelf

Product edits & dupes — shoppable, tasteful, curated not exhaustive.

Seoul Dispatch

On-the-ground reporting — Olive Young drops, Seongsu pop-ups, clinic culture. The Seoul-native moat.

The Verdict

Marketing claim vs. actual evidence, MD-checked, one paragraph — the credibility garnish.

City Guides

The medical-tourism decision layer: Seoul clinic guides, price bands, what to book — the differentiator.

MD credibility is a light trust layer on a beauty magazine, never the main course. The moat is Seoul-native sourcing + taste + the decision layer.

03 · Why now / Moat

Why now, and why it’s defensible.

  • The super-cycle is now. Record exports, US #1, Japan #1, a medical-tourism boom, and a billionaire minted on the Rejuran run — the English/JP/CN audience for exactly this subject is expanding fast.
  • Discovery is the scarce good. Xiaohongshu proves curated discovery — not raw product — is what beauty buyers pay attention to; someone has to translate Seoul with taste.
  • Solo + AI makes a Seoul-native magazine viable. AI-assisted production + one Seoul contributor now delivers a multi-market publication that used to need a newsroom.

The moat

This category exits — Highsnobiety→Zalando, Byrdie→Dotdash, Dazed runs a studio + membership. But Seoul-native sourcing + a taste-driven premium voice + a light MD trust layer + a medical-tourism decision layer is a position none of the incumbents hold. Own the newsletter and membership as the durable, platform-independent asset.

11 · The ask

Bootstrap a media brand — a raise is the exception, not the rule.

Content-commerce media is not a clean VC scale story: Highsnobiety raised ~$10.2M before a strategic exit; Air Mail’s $17M is the exception. Glassy is profitable on affiliate + native + membership at a solo + AI + one-contributor cost base.

  • Recommended: bootstrap to profitability, keep the cost base lean, retain full control and editorial independence.
  • Optional strategic check ($150–300k) only after the brand-studio and membership lines are proven — ideally from a beauty-commerce or medical-tourism strategic, not a growth fund.
  • The exit context: this category is acquired — Highsnobiety→Zalando, Byrdie→Dotdash, and beauty/K-beauty strategics are hungry for a trusted, multi-market Seoul-native brand.
