# Glassy v3 — Three-Concept Build Brief
### A premium online magazine for Korean beauty & dermatology *trends*, curated with taste for the US / Japan / China

> **The plan:** build **THREE distinct site + business-model versions of Glassy**, each reverse-engineered from a *different, currently-successful* business, chosen so their **monetization engines do not overlap**:
> - **Concept A — "Glassy Shelf"** ← **Soko Glam / The Klog** → *commerce-led editorial (curated e-commerce + own-brand)*
> - **Concept B — "Glassy Radar"** ← **Hypebae / Hypebeast** → *fast trend media + brand studio (native/sponsorship) + commerce*
> - **Concept C — "Glassy Dispatch"** ← **Air Mail** → *premium paid membership + luxury advertising*
>
> Shared across all three: the market, the audience, the benchmark rates, the risks, and one bolt-on differentiator (a **medical-tourism referral/decision layer**) — all defined once below. The MD/clinical angle is a **light trust layer** ("MD-checked Verdict"), never a medical-evidence journal, and never the dark/technical SemiAnalysis look.

Date: 2026-07-07 · Hard numbers carry source URLs. Modeled figures are labeled **(est.)**.

# SHARED FOUNDATION (applies to all three concepts)

## S.1 The white space (why any version wins)
Korean beauty & derm are in a **global demand super-cycle**, yet no one owns a **premium, taste-driven, multi-market (US/JP/CN) K-beauty & derm *TREND* magazine with Seoul-native sourcing + a light MD trust layer + a medical-tourism decision layer.** Existing players are either shops-with-a-blog (Soko Glam), Western generalists (Byrdie/Allure), loud culture-media (Hypebae/Dazed), or single-voice newsletters (Substack). The refined, Seoul-native, multi-market trend desk is unclaimed.

## S.2 Market sizing (TAM / SAM / SOM) — cited
**TAM (demand pool Glassy sits on):**
- Global **beauty & personal care ≈ US$559–593B (2024)**; online BPC alone **≈ US$418B (2024)**. → https://www.precedenceresearch.com/beauty-and-personal-care-products-market
- **K-beauty** (broad "products market") **US$118.3B (2025) → US$252.4B (2033), 10% CAGR** (Grand View; note narrower "Korean-origin" definitions run ~$11–16B). → https://www.grandviewresearch.com/press-release/global-k-beauty-products-market
- **Reality anchor (hard govt data):** Korea's cosmetics exports = **US$10.2B (2024, +20.3% YoY), #3 worldwide**; top buyers **China $2.5B · US $1.9B · Japan $1.0B** = Glassy's three markets. → https://www.korea.net/NewsFocus/Business/view?articleId=272319
- **US K-beauty product market: US$27.56B (2024) → US$55.12B (2032), 9.05% CAGR**; Korea became the **#1 cosmetics import source into the US in 2024**; Amazon K-beauty **+78% YoY** (2023). → https://www.credenceresearch.com/report/united-states-k-beauty-product-market · https://www.cosmeticsdesign.com/Article/2024/10/16/riding-the-second-wave-of-k-beauty-in-the-us-market/

**SAM (money a media property can touch):**
- **Global influencer/creator marketing ≈ US$24B (2024) → US$32.55B (2025)**; **beauty influencers alone ≈ US$4.5B**. → https://www.factorypr.com/how-big-is-the-influencer-marketing-industry-right-now/
- **Beauty advertising ≈ US$7.7B/yr**. → https://labelservice.co.uk/summary-of-the-global-industry-beauty-statistics-2024/
- **Medical-tourism referral pool:** 705,044 foreign **dermatology** patients (2024) × avg package (est. $1–5k) × 10–20% facilitator fee → a **US$100M+** skimmable pool.

**SOM (bottom-up reachable, 3 yrs, est.):** English + JP + CN K-beauty-trend readers. Comparable niche premium reach: Byrdie+MyDomaine did **5.5M monthly uniques**; Air Mail built **~US$15M** on a narrow affluent audience. Glassy Y3 target: **~0.7–1.0M monthly uniques + 40–60k newsletter subs**, converting to **~US$0.9–1.4M/yr** depending on concept (models in each section).

## S.3 Audience (all three concepts)
- **US:** K-beauty gone mainstream (Amazon/Sephora/Olive Young US); millennial + Gen-Z women, English-first, discovery-hungry.
- **Japan:** Korea **#1 in cosmetics imports 3 straight years (2022–2024)**; Korean brands sweep **Qoo10** bestsellers. → https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10723199
- **China:** **Xiaohongshu 300M MAU**, and **87% of Chinese beauty users treat it as their primary discovery source** — CN must be **social-first (Xiaohongshu/WeChat)**, not open web. → https://cosmeticschinaagency.com/87-of-beauty-users-in-china-use-little-red-book-primary-source-for-discovering-new-brands/

## S.5 The shared differentiator — medical-tourism decision layer
Any of the three concepts bolts on **City Guides**: MD-checked "where to go" guidance for the 705k/yr foreign derm patients, monetized by **KHIDI-registered** clinic referral fees (10–20%). No beauty magazine currently captures this. **Must be fully disclosed and firewalled from the editorial "Verdict."**

## S.6 Shared risks
- **Trust vs. sponsorship:** the MD "Verdict" and referrals **cannot be for sale**; hard editorial firewall + FTC/affiliate/referral disclosure.
- **Trend shelf-life:** "렉카" velocity content decays; pair with **evergreen derm/City-Guide SEO annuities**.
- **Multi-language ops / China:** CJK typography + localization cost; **China = Xiaohongshu/WeChat, ICP + content compliance, account-ban risk**; phase CN last, social-first.
- **Platform dependence:** SEO/IG/TikTok/Xiaohongshu algo risk → **own the newsletter list + membership**.
- **Medical & ad regulation:** derm claims, Korea medical-advertising law, cross-border referral ethics → MD review, "info not medical advice" framing, registered partners only.
- **Solo bandwidth:** one founder can't cover Seoul reporting + 3 languages + sales → AI-assisted production + 1 Seoul contributor + syndication before headcount.

## S.7 Shared unit-economics assumptions *(est., solo + AI + 1 part-time Seoul contributor)*
Blended programmatic RPM ~$12 (premium beauty); direct native package ~$15k avg (vs $55k Air Mail anchor — Glassy is smaller); affiliate ~10% commission, ~1% reader→buy at ~$60 AOV; membership ~$70/yr; referral avg ~$450/patient ($3k × 15%). Cash cost base: contributor $2–4k/mo, tools/AI/hosting $0.5–1.5k/mo, shoots/licensing variable → ~$35k (Y1) → ~$170k (Y3).

# CONCEPT A — "GLASSY SHELF"
### reverse-engineered from **Soko Glam / The Klog** · model = **commerce-led editorial**

**Positioning (one line):** *"The curated K-beauty counter for the world — taste-filtered shelves, MD-checked, shoppable in one tap."*

## A.1 The business & why it works
Soko Glam (Charlotte & Dave Cho, launched 2012) is the **largest Korean-beauty site in the US**, built on **curation as the product** (~**110 brands**, not everything). It paired a shop with an editorial arm, **The Klog**, that taught the "10-step routine" and *created the demand it then sold into*. Public signals of durability: **$2M seed (2017)**, **profitable since year 2**, self-funded (no pressure to chase newness), and it climbed the value chain into **own brands** (Then I Met You, 2018; The Klog line, 2020). → https://www.bustle.com/p/soko-glams-charlotte-cho-has-already-upended-the-beauty-industry-its-only-the-start-18141572 · https://wwd.com/beauty-industry-news/beauty-features/charlotte-cho-digital-the-perfect-k-beauty-platform-1202561834/

## A.2 Business model reverse-engineered → Glassy adaptation
**Revenue stack (how it stacks):**
1. **Retail/curation margin** — core; buy/curate K-beauty, sell at retail margin (typically 40–55% gross on beauty). *Glassy-light alternative if not holding inventory:* run it as **affiliate curation** first (Sephora 5–10%, LTK/ShopMy 10–30% — see S.4), upgrade to owned inventory only on proven winners.
2. **Editorial → product funnel** — The Klog content ("Charlotte's Curation Picks," "Best of K-Beauty Awards") converts readers to buyers; content is the **top of a commerce funnel**, not a standalone P&L.
3. **Own-brand ladder** — once a taste-authority exists, launch a **Glassy house edit / house brand** (highest margin, Soko Glam's Then I Met You playbook).
4. **Bolt-on:** City Guides referral layer (S.5).
**Growth loop:** taste-curated content → SEO/social discovery → shop conversion → reviews/awards → more trust → stronger curation authority → own-brand pricing power.
**Glassy adaptation:** the shop is a **tight, tasteful "Shelf"** of what's trending in Seoul *this month* (not an endless catalog) — each SKU carries a Trend Radar rating + a one-line MD "Verdict." Multi-market: US ships/affiliates via Amazon+Sephora; JP via Qoo10-aware edits; CN as Xiaohongshu shoppable lookbook.
**3-yr headline (est.):** commerce-led ramps slowest to profit but highest ceiling — Y1 ~$60k, Y2 ~$350k, **Y3 ~$1.1M** (blend of affiliate + first owned-brand + referral); needs working capital if inventory is held.

## A.3 Site/design reverse-engineered — clone the structure · **https://sokoglam.com**
- **Typography:** **sans-serif throughout** (clean modern grotesque), plain descriptive headlines ("20% Off Top K-Beauty Best Sellers"), no decorative serifs; clean product names. *For Glassy, lift the type but nudge more premium (tighter tracking, one editorial serif for feature headlines).*
- **Color/accent:** neutral **white/off-white** ground + a **single saturated brand accent** (Soko Glam uses indigo/purple `#574CD5` for nav, buttons, logo) + seasonal pink/blue promo accents.
- **Layout/grid:** **mega-menu** nav (Shop All · Discover K-Beauty · Skin Care by type · Brands · **The Klog** · 10-Step Routine) → **hero promo banner** → **product carousels** ("Newly Curated," "Best Sellers," "Viral K-Beauty") → **"Start Your K-Beauty Journey"** tabbed block → **6-tile category grid** (Cleansers/Toners/Essence/Moisturizers/SPF/Masks) → **lifestyle editorial blocks** → **testimonials + press ("Best of K-Beauty Awards")** → **Instagram** strip.
- **Imagery:** clean, well-lit, **consistent-angle product photography** + relatable lifestyle shots; high-contrast responsive banners.
- **IA / commerce-editorial blend:** **shop-first with The Klog as the editorial engine**; "Watch & Shop" video-commerce; editor-curated lists position editors as trusted guides. *Glassy clones this exact hierarchy: Shelf (shop) primary, "The Klog"→"Glassy Journal" secondary, curator-as-authority throughout.*
- **Name + positioning:** **Glassy Shelf** — "the curated K-beauty counter for the world."

# CONCEPT B — "GLASSY RADAR"
### reverse-engineered from **Hypebae / Hypebeast** · model = **trend media + brand studio + commerce**

**Positioning (one line):** *"Seoul's beauty & derm trends at internet speed — premium trend media with a brand studio."*

## B.1 The business & why it works
Hypebae is the female beauty/fashion trend vertical of **Hypebeast Ltd (HKEX: 0150)** — a **public** trend-media company that turned fast cultural coverage into a **native-advertising + commerce** machine. FY2024 reported **total revenue HK$895.7M (~US$115M, est. FX)**, of which the **Media segment = HK$608.3M** at **46.2% gross margin**; the group also runs **HBX** commerce. → https://hypebeast.com/newsroom/hypebeast-ltd-0150-hk-reports-2024-half-year-financial-results · The model is proven at scale by peers too: **Highsnobiety >US$60M/yr, ~half e-commerce, acquired by Zalando** precisely because *editorial drives commerce*. → https://www.glossy.co/fashion/highsnobiety-acquired-by-zalando-showing-the-power-of-editorial-to-drive-commerce/

## B.2 Business model reverse-engineered → Glassy adaptation
**Revenue stack:**
1. **Display + programmatic** — high-velocity pageviews monetized at cosmetics CPM ~$11.6, N. America $15–20 (S.4).
2. **Brand studio / native sponsorship** — the profit center: brands pay for editorial-grade native packages (~$15k Glassy / $55k Air Mail anchor). Beauty brands are pouring into this — **beauty influencer/native pool ≈ $4.5B** (S.2).
3. **Commerce** — a shoppable layer / "Shop the Radar" (HBX analog) via affiliate first, owned drops later.
4. **Consulting/insight** — Highsnobiety's high-margin arm: sell **Seoul trend intelligence** to Western/JP/CN brands (Glassy sits on proprietary trend-velocity data).
5. **Bolt-on:** City Guides referral (S.5).
**Growth loop:** fast trend coverage → social virality (IG/TikTok/Xiaohongshu) → traffic scale → audience data → brand-studio + insight sales → funds more/faster coverage.
**Glassy adaptation:** the **Trend Radar** desk is the homepage engine — dated, ranked, "should-you-care" cards on what's blowing up in Seoul; brand-studio work is labeled + firewalled from the MD Verdict. Multi-market: same trend, three localized takes (US/JP/CN).
**3-yr headline (est.):** fastest to revenue via native — Y1 ~$70k, Y2 ~$450k, **Y3 ~$1.2M** (programmatic + 20 native packages + affiliate + first insight retainers).

## B.3 Site/design reverse-engineered — clone the structure · **https://hypebae.com**
- **Typography:** **sans-serif throughout**, **sentence-case headlines, moderate weight**, sized for fast scanning. *For Glassy, keep the sans engine but add a premium display cut for hero features so it reads tasteful, not tabloid.*
- **Color/accent:** **neutral** (white bg, dark text) with accent used **sparingly** for interactive elements/commerce (Hypecoins). Restraint = premium.
- **Layout/grid:** **card-based feed grid** — a **"Most Hyped"** module up top → **paginated latest feed**; every card = **thumbnail + headline + byline + relative timestamp ("1 Hr ago") + engagement metrics**; **large full-width hero** for the featured story; a **trending-topics widget**.
- **Imagery:** big full-width hero images; **consistent aspect-ratio thumbnails** enabling rapid visual scanning.
- **IA:** top nav by section (**Fashion · Footwear · Beauty · Sports · Art&Design · Music · Culture · Radio**) + **More** + **Store → HBX**. *Glassy IA: Trend Radar · The Treatment (derm) · The Shelf (products) · Seoul Dispatch · The Verdict · City Guides + Shop.*
- **Commerce:** store as a **distinct linked property** (HBX: Men/Women/Life/Golf/Archives). *Glassy: a linked "Shop the Radar" affiliate/commerce surface.*
- **Name + positioning:** **Glassy Radar** — "Seoul beauty & derm trends at internet speed."

# CONCEPT C — "GLASSY DISPATCH"
### reverse-engineered from **Air Mail** · model = **premium paid membership + luxury advertising**

**Positioning (one line):** *"The weekly letter from Seoul's beauty frontier — members only, impeccably curated."*

## C.1 The business & why it works
Air Mail (Graydon Carter + Alessandra Stanley, 2019) is a **mobile-first premium digital weekly** — "the best weekend edition of your favorite paper" — that monetizes a **narrow, affluent, taste-led audience** rather than mass traffic. It brought in **>US$15M annual revenue (2023)** from a clean three-way stack, raised **$17M (2021)**, and was **acquired by Puck (Sept 2025)** — validating the model. → https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_Mail_(magazine) · https://wwd.com/business-news/media/graydon-carter-air-mail-stores-cafe-armie-hammer-canceled-men-1235821714/

## C.2 Business model reverse-engineered → Glassy adaptation
**Revenue stack (Air Mail's actual mix):**
1. **Subscription/membership** — **$79.99/yr** standard + **$300/yr "Editor's Circle"**; **~90% of trials convert**. *Glassy: ~$70/yr membership — ad-light reading, full City Guides, archive, "Seoul concierge" perks.*
2. **Luxury advertising = ~42% of revenue** — a single issue placement ~**$55,000**, mostly luxury brands who want the affluent context (beauty/derm luxury maps perfectly).
3. **Commerce ≈ 15%** — the **Air Supply** shop: editor-curated products (incl. exclusive branded items) sold through editorial framing. *Glassy: "The Supply" — curated Seoul-only finds + affiliate.*
4. **Bolt-on:** City Guides referral (S.5) fits naturally into a members' concierge tier.
**Growth loop:** distinctive editorial voice + scarcity → paid trials → 90% convert → loyal affluent base → luxury advertisers pay premium for that base → funds better editorial → stronger voice.
**Glassy adaptation:** a **weekly members' Dispatch from Seoul** (the taste-and-authority play), luxury K-beauty/derm/clinic advertisers, a small curated shop, and a concierge tier that quietly includes medical-tourism guidance. Lowest traffic requirement, highest per-reader value; slowest to scale but most defensible.
**3-yr headline (est.):** Y1 ~$45k, Y2 ~$380k, **Y3 ~$0.95M** (3–4k members × $70 + luxury ads + supply + concierge referrals).

## C.3 Site/design reverse-engineered — clone the structure · **https://airmail.news**
- **Typography:** **serif headlines with substantial weight** (editorial authority), **sans-serif body** for readability, **title case**; bylines linked to named contributors. *This is the "premium magazine" type system — clone it closely.*
- **Color/accent:** **black on white, minimal**, with a **distinctive red/crimson accent** for branding + CTAs ("Subscribe," "Read On").
- **Layout/grid:** **magazine-style** — a **prominent featured story hero** with big imagery → **curated grid organized by section** → the **latest issue (No. 364) featured with cover imagery** → secondary **card grids** (thumbnail + headline + byline), consistent image sizing for rhythm.
- **Imagery:** high-quality **editorial photography placed above headlines**; uniform sizing creates visual cadence.
- **IA:** primary nav **News · Arts & Letters · Style & Living**, each with subsections; departments incl. **Fashion & Beauty**, Travel, Food & Drink, Design; signature columns ("The View from Here," "Arts Intel"), a podcast ("The Morning Meeting"). *Glassy: Seoul Dispatch · The Treatment · The Shelf · The Verdict · City Guides, published as a dated weekly Issue.*
- **Paywall:** persistent **Subscribe / Sign In / Gift** prompts; premium content gated. **Air Supply** shop given **equal nav prominence**, collections shown in editorial frames.
- **Name + positioning:** **Glassy Dispatch** — "the weekly letter from Seoul's beauty frontier, members only."

# DECISION MATRIX (which to build / sequence)
| | **A · Glassy Shelf** | **B · Glassy Radar** | **C · Glassy Dispatch** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reference | Soko Glam / The Klog | Hypebae / Hypebeast | Air Mail |
| Primary revenue | Commerce margin / affiliate → own-brand | Native/brand-studio + commerce | Membership + luxury ads |
| Traffic needed | Medium (buyers) | **High** (scale) | **Low** (affluent few) |
| Speed to revenue | Slow (funnel/inventory) | **Fast** (native) | Medium (build trust first) |
| Ceiling | **Highest** (own-brand) | High | Medium-High |
| Capital need | **Highest** (inventory) | Low | Low |
| Design register | Bright, product-forward, sans | Fast card-grid, sans, image-led | Serif, editorial, restrained |
| Best if founder wants… | to build a K-beauty commerce brand | to build a scaled trend-media brand | to build a defensible premium franchise |

**Suggested sequence (est.):** launch **B (Radar)** first for fastest cashflow + audience, layer **C (Dispatch)** membership onto the trusted audience, and spin **A (Shelf)** commerce/own-brand once curation authority is proven — but each is designed to stand alone as a co-equal build.
