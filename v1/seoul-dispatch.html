Seoul Dispatch — 5 Treatments Trending in Gangnam This Quarter

What's actually being booked right now, straight from the source — with a note on which ones are worth the airfare.

Glassy Edit · 4 min · Jul 2026

Every quarter we take the temperature of what Seoul's clinics are actually booking — not what's being advertised, what's being scheduled. Here are the five moving right now, each with a one-line verdict and a "fly-in factor."

1 — Polynucleotide boosters (Rejuran & family)

Still the anchor of the glass-skin stack. Barrier and hydration, defined mechanism, the sturdiest evidence in the booster aisle. Fly-in factor: high — best access and pricing in Korea.

2 — Multi-point HIFU (Shrink Universe, Ultraformer MPT)

The "no-surgery lift" that's fully booked. Real for early laxity, oversold as a facelift. Fly-in factor: high — device range + volume + price.

3 — Exosome add-ons

The upsell of the moment — and the one with the widest evidence gap. Regulators are pushing back (the word itself is banned in Korean cosmetic ads). Fly-in factor: low — you're paying the most for the least proof.

4 — RF microneedling

Texture, pores, mild tightening. Workhorse, not a headline. Widely available at home. Fly-in factor: medium — fine to do locally with a good operator.

5 — "Skin-quality" toxins (microtox / skinbotox)

Micro-dosed neurotoxin for pore and oil control, the "poreless" finish. Trending hard; keep the dose light. Fly-in factor: medium — technique-dependent; find the operator, not the city.

The dispatch read

Two of the five (PDRN boosters, HIFU) genuinely reward a Seoul trip on access and price. One (exosomes) is the quarter's most-upsold and least-evidenced. The rest travel fine. Book the flight for the layers Seoul does better — not for the ones every good clinic already does.

Indicative and observational, as of Jul 2026. Educational — not medical advice; no specific clinic promotion.
